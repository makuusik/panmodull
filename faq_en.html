How is thermal insulation provided?

To ensure a comfortable temperature inside the house And reduce energy consumption for heating and cooling, we use high-density rock wool with a thickness of 250 mm in the walls and 200 mm in the floor. Inside the house, a vapor barrier is used 150 microns thick with sealing of all joints for full airtightness. The outer layer consists of a membrane that protects against moisture, preventing its penetration from the outside, while allowing condensation to drain from inside the insulation. This ensures a healthy environment and the durability of the construction.

What can the facade be like?

When choosing the exterior facade of a frame house, you have two main options: ventilated and non-ventilated.

Ventilated facade:
- Materials: wood and metal.
- Features: Provides air circulation between the material facade material and the wall, which helps remove moisture and prevents formation of mold and condensation. This type of facade promotes the longevity of the structure and improves the microclimate inside the house.

Non-ventilated facade:
- Materials: mineral wool and facade plaster.
- Features: This type of facade seals the walls tightly, providing excellent thermal and acoustic insulation. Facade unventilated is more often chosen to ensure maximum energy efficiency and minimization of heat loss heat.

The choice between ventilated and non-ventilated facades depends on Your preferences and priorities for insulation, durability and the exterior appearance of the building.

What is the heating system?

Frame houses can be equipped with different heating systems, each of which has its own advantages and is suitable for different conditions and owners' preferences. Here are the main options for systems heating systems that can be installed in a frame house:

1. electric heating:
-Electric Convectors: Simple to install, No pipe laying required. Ideal for small houses or as additional source of heat.
-Infrared heaters: They heat objects and surfaces rather than air, which ensures even heat distribution and energy savings.
-Underfloor heating: Electric underfloor heating systems provide comfortable warmth and can be used as a main or supplementary heating source.

2. gas heating:
-Gas boilers: A popular and effective option for frame houses. Gas boilers can be condensing or traditional and can run on natural or liquefied gas.
-Underfloor heating: Systems Water floor heating systems, connected to a gas boiler, provide even and comfortable heat.

3 Liquid fuel systems:
-Liquid fuel boilers: They can be used in Areas where there is no access to natural gas. These boilers operate on diesel or mazut oil and can be integrated with radiators or underfloor heating systems.

4. solid fuel systems:
-Wood or coal-fired boilers: Suitable for Rural areas, they provide autonomous heating. They can be connected to a radiator or underfloor heating system.
-Stoves and fireplaces: In addition, they create a cozy atmosphere in the house and can be used as the main or additional source of heat.

5. heat pumps:
-Air-to-air: They extract heat from the Outside air and transfer it to the house. Effective in temperate climates.
-Air-to-Water: They extract heat from the outside air Outside air and transfer it to a water heating system, such as radiators or underfloor heating.
-Land-Water: They extract heat from the ground via Using underground pipes and transferring it to the water heating system water heating. Most energy efficient, but requires significant initial investment.

6 Hybrid systems:
-A combination of different sources: For example The use of a heat pump in combination with a gas boiler or an electric heater. This allows you to optimize energy consumption energy and ensure reliable heating in all conditions.

Recommendations for selecting a heating system:
1.Take into account climatic conditions: The important thing is, to choose a system that will be efficient in your region. For cold winters, gas boilers or heat pumps are better suited ground-to-water.
2. Consider the availability of energy resources: The choice of system depends on the availability of natural gas, energy electricity or other fuels in your area.
3.Evaluate energy consumption and cost-effectiveness: Important is to take into account both the initial installation costs and the and the ongoing operating costs of the heating system.
4. Consult with professionals: Contact Specialists to get a consultation and develop a heating system design, taking into account the specifics of your house frame house.

Panmodul is ready to help you choose and install the The optimal heating system for your frame house, ensuring the comfort and energy efficiency of your dwelling.
